# Full-Text Availability Note

## Preserved fact

- 13 records were excluded because their full texts could not be retrieved.
- This corresponds to 31.0% of the post-deduplication set (`13 / 42`).
- Relative to the terminal-focused final retained corpus, this loss is 41.9% of the final sample size (`13 / 31`).

## Why this matters

This is an availability-based attrition, not a topical exclusion. It therefore introduces a plausible accessibility bias into the review.

## What the current workspace preserves

- the aggregate count of 13 inaccessible full-text exclusions;
- the source collections used in Zotero;
- the final retained extraction sheet.

## What the current workspace does not preserve

- the authoritative article-level list of those 13 records;
- a per-record retrieval log showing which access attempts failed;
- a clean separation between inaccessible records, topical exclusions, duplicate versions, and later replacements.

## Local reconstruction attempt

On March 18, 2026, the local Zotero workspace was inspected to see whether a definitive full-text-loss appendix could be reconstructed from the preserved collections. That inspection confirmed the existence of source collections and a curated retained-study collection, but it did not yield a one-to-one, authoritative mapping for the 13 inaccessible records. The non-retained records preserved in Zotero mix multiple cases:

- studies outside the final topical scope;
- review papers or less-complete versions;
- records that appear in source collections without a preserved PDF;
- records that were later replaced by more complete accessible entries.

## Practical consequence

The manuscript can now report the magnitude of the accessibility loss transparently, but it still cannot claim a definitive appendix listing the 13 inaccessible full-text studies. In the current journal-facing version, this accessibility loss is reported against a terminal-focused final corpus of 31 studies after removing three peripheral non-terminal analogues from the broader quality-passing set. Recovering the original Parsifal screening log remains necessary before journal submission.
